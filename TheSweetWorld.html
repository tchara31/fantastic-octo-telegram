Ingredients
3 cups old-fashioned rolled oats (see Tip)
1.25 cups low-fat milk
0.5 cup unsweetened applesauce
0.66 cup packed light brown sugar
1 tablespoon grated lemon zest
0.25 cup lemon juice
2 large eggs, lightly beaten
1 teaspoon baking powder
1 teaspoon vanilla extract
0.5 teaspoon salt
1 cup frozen blueberries, preferably wild

Recipe
1. Preheat oven to 375 degrees Fahrenheit.
2. Combine oats, milk, applesauce, brown sugar, lemon zests, lemon juice, eggs, baking powder, vanilla, and salt in a large bowl.
3. Fold in the frozen blueberries.
4. Divide the mixture greased muffin cups.
5. Bake for about 25 minutes.
6. Cool on a wire rack and enjoy!

Ingredients
4 ounces salmon, preferably wild
1 teaspoon avocado oil
0.125 teaspoon kosher salt
1 cup instant brown rice
1 cup water
2 tablespoons mayonnaise
1.5 teaspoons Sriracha
1.5 teaspoons 50%-less-sodium tamari
1 teaspoon mirin
0.5 teaspoon freshly grated ginger
0.25 teaspoon crushed red pepper
0.125 teaspoon kosher salt
0.5 ripe avocado, chopped
0.5 cup chopped cucumber
0.25 cup spicy kimchi
12 (4-inch) sheets nori (roasted seaweed)

Recipe
1. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees Fahrenheit.
2. Line a small baking sheet with foil and place salmon on it.
3. Bake for 8 to 10 minutes.
4. Meanwhile, cook rice in a small saucepan.
5. Mix mayonnaise and sriracha and set aside.
6. Whisk tamari, mirin, ginger, crushed red pepper and salt in another small bowl and set aside.
7. Top rice with the two mixtures and the salmon.
8. Serve with nori.

Ingredients
2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
8 ounces sliced fresh mixed wild mushrooms (cremini, shiitake, button, or oyster mushrooms)
1.5 cups thinly sliced sweet onion
1 tablespoon thinly sliced garlic
5 ounces fresh baby spinach (about 8 cups), coarsely chopped
6 large eggs
0.25 cup whole milk
0.25 cup half-and-half
1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
1 tablespoon fresh thyme leaves, plus more for garnish
0.25 teaspoon salt
0.25 teaspoon ground pepper
1.5 cups shredded Gruyère cheese

Recipe
1. Preheat oven to 375 degrees Fahrenheit.
2. Coat a 9-inch pie pan with cooking spray.
3. Heat oil in a large nonstick skillet.
4. Add mushrooms to oil and cook, stirring occasionally, for about 8 minutes.
5. Add onions and garlic and stir for around 5 minutes.
6. Add spinach and toss until completely wilted.
7. Remove from heat.
8. Whisk eggs, milk, half-and-half, thyme, salt, and pepper in a medium bowl.
9. Fold in the mushroom mixture and cheese.
10. Spoon into the prepared pie pan and bake until golden brown (~30 minutes).
11. Let the disdh cool, and serve!

Ingredients
Medium serrano pepper, cut into thirds
4 large cloves garlic
1 2-inch piece fresh ginger, peeled and coarsely chopped
1 medium yellow onion, chopped (1-inch)
6 tablespoons canola oil or grapeseed oil
2 teaspoons ground coriander
2 teaspoons ground cumin
0.5 teaspoon ground turmeric
2.25 cups no-salt-added canned diced tomatoes with their juice (from a 28-ounce can)
0.75 teaspoon kosher salt
2 15-ounce cans chickpeas, rinsed
2 teaspoons garam masala
Fresh cilantro for garnish

Recipe
1. Pulse serrano, garlic, and ginger in a food processor until minced. Scrape down the sides and pulse again. Add onion; pulse until finely chopped, but not watery.
2. Heat oil in a large saucepan over medium-high heat.
3. Add the onion mixture and cook, stirring occasionally, until softened, 3 to 5 minutes.
4. Add coriander, cumin and turmeric and cook, stirring, for 2 minutes.
5. Pulse tomatoes in the food processor until finely chopped.
6. Add to the pan along with salt; reduce heat to maintain a simmer and cook, stirring occasionally, for 4 minutes.
7. Add chickpeas and garam masala, reduce heat to a gentle simmer, cover and cook, stirring occasionally, for 5 minutes more.
8. Serve topped with cilantro, if desired.



Our Mission:
Our mission here at The Sweet Life is to aid diabetic and obese patients, who are not sure where to start on their journey of recovery. From 2017 to 2020, 42% of Americans were obese, 15 million of those cases being life-threatening, and that number has only increased since then. Though most people would say they want to recover and live a healthier lifestyle, they often find themselves unsure where to start, ultimately giving up on their chance at a better life. We hope our website can serve as a platform to kickstart the road to healthier habits by discovering places nearby to shop for fresh produce, and new healthy recipes to implement into a balanced diet.
